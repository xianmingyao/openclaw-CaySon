# PM Heuristics

## Ship The Insight, Not The Feature

Features are outputs. Insight is understanding what job matters, why current solutions fail, and where willingness to pay lives. So what: product scope should follow customer truth, not roadmap momentum.

## If You Cannot Describe The Customer, You Do Not Have A Market

A market description should name the buyer, context, pain, and trigger. "Consumers" is not a segment. So what: vague customers create vague products and expensive acquisition.

## Revenue Model Determines Product Architecture

Pricing and revenue mechanics shape onboarding, packaging, retention loops, and support needs. So what: if the business model fights the product design, one of them loses.

## The Feature That Takes One Day To Build And One Year To Support

Always ask what the support, QA, operations, and edge-case burden will be. So what: maintenance cost often kills the ROI of "easy" features.

## Competition Is A Signal, Not A Threat

Competitors validate demand. The real question is whether the market is crowded because it is valuable or crowded because differentiation is weak. So what: competition should sharpen your wedge, not scare you into vagueness.

## Distribution Beats Product

A very good product with weak distribution often loses to a good product with strong channel, trust, and reach. So what: go-to-market is part of the product strategy, not an afterthought.

## Kill It Before It Kills You

Sunk costs make mediocre bets feel salvageable. Explicit kill criteria protect time, capital, and credibility. So what: discipline is a strategic advantage.
