# Positioning

Positioning is the shortcut customers use to decide what your product is, who it is for, and why it matters. If you cannot state that clearly, the market will do it for you.

## Positioning Statement

Use the formula:

For [target], [product] is the [category] that [differentiator] because [reasons to believe].

Each field must be concrete:

- Target: narrow enough to picture.
- Category: familiar enough to orient the buyer.
- Differentiator: outcome, not feature dump.
- Reasons to believe: proof points that make the claim credible.

So what: a good statement filters customers as much as it attracts them.

## Category Creation Vs Category Entry

Create a new category when:

- Existing categories misdescribe the product.
- The new framing changes buying criteria in your favor.
- You have enough evidence and budget to teach the market.

Enter an existing category when:

- Buyers already know how to shop.
- You can win within the current frame.
- Education cost would be too high.

So what: category creation is powerful, but it is expensive and slow.

## Positioning Traps

- Trying to be for everyone.
- Positioning on features instead of outcomes.
- Claiming uniqueness without proof.
- Using language customers do not use.
- Confusing internal strategy language with external messaging.

So what: unclear positioning increases CAC because the buyer must do the interpretation work.

## Competitive Positioning

Choose the frame deliberately:

- Direct competitor frame when you want explicit comparison and can win on known criteria.
- Adjacent frame when direct comparison is unfavorable or too limiting.

Ask:

- Which comparison makes us look strongest?
- Which comparison do customers already make?
- What frame do channels and reviewers use?

So what: the frame determines the scoreboard.

## Message Testing

Validate before launch:

- Concept tests with target buyers.
- Ad/message experiments with response by segment.
- Sales calls or interviews using alternative narratives.
- Landing pages with different positioning variants.

Measure:

- Comprehension.
- Relevance.
- Distinctiveness.
- Purchase intent.

So what: the best positioning is the one customers repeat back accurately.
